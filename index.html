Hangzhou Impressions

Hángzhōu’s (杭州), the capital of China’s Zhejiang province, is the southern terminus of the ancient Grand Canal waterway, which originates in Beijing. Its West Lake, celebrated by poets and artists since the 9th century, encompasses islands (reachable by boat), temples, pavilions, gardens and arched bridges. On its south bank is 5-story Leifeng Pagoda, a modern reconstruction of a structure built in 975 A.D.

Discover Hangzhou

Geography

Located in northwestern Zhejiang province, Hangzhou is in the south-central portion of the Yangtze River Delta. It extends west to the mountainous parts of Anhui province, and east to the coastal plain near Hangzhou Bay.

Learn more

History

Hangzhou is one of China's seven ancient capitals and designated a "Historic and Cultural City". Its history can be traced back over 2,200 years to the Qin dynasty (221-206 BC). It flourished after being linked with the north via the Grand Canal, which starts in Beijing and ends in Hangzhou.

Food

Food in Hangzhou is characterized by its sweet and light flavors, enjoyable presentation, and delicate cooking methods. The most famous Hangzhou dishes include West Lake fish in sweet and sour sauce, shelled shrimp with Dragon Well tea, and Dongpo pork.
