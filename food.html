Food

Food in Hangzhou is characterized by its sweet and light flavors, enjoyable presentation, and delicate cooking methods. The most famous Hangzhou dishes include West Lake fish in sweet and sour sauce, shelled shrimp with Dragon Well tea, and Dongpo pork.

Fried Shrimp with Longjing Tea (龙井虾仁) is a traditional food in Hangzhou. Stir-fry fresh river shrimp with Longjing tea, tea leaves, and rice wine. The fresh fragrance of Longjing tea and the shrimp blend together. The dish tastes fragrant and light.

Dongpo Pork (东坡肉) is a very popular food among tourists and locals alike. It is named after a famous poet, Su Dongpo, who is credited with the creation of the dish. To cook this dish, pork belly is braised in a sealed pot for hours. When finished, the pork is flavorful and melts in your mouth.

Victory Cake (定胜糕), which originated during the Song Dynasty, is very popular with locals. It is made from delicate glutinous rice flour and has a filling of bean paste mixed with white sugar and osmanthus flowers. Victory Cake is fragrant, lightly sweet, and deliciously glutinous.
