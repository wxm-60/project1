History

Hangzhou is one of China's seven ancient capitals and designated a "Historic and Cultural City". Its history can be traced back over 2,200 years to the Qin dynasty (221-206 BC). It flourished after being linked with the north via the Grand Canal, which starts in Beijing and ends in Hangzhou.

Select Highlights in the History of Hangzhou

  • Prior to 10th century: Lingyin temple founded in 328 CE
  • 10th century: City became capital of Wuyue Kingdom in 904
  • 12th-17th century: Song Dynasty capital relocated to Hangzhou in 1127; poulation 1.75million
  • 19th century: City opens to foreign trade per Treaty of Shimonoseki in 1896
  • 20th century: Hangzhou Hi-Tech Industrial Development Zone approved in 1991
  • 21st century: Hosted G20 Summit in 2016
